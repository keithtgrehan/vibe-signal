# Legal Safe Output Policy

Some users explore changes in communication patterns when trying to understand relationship dynamics.

VibeSignal AI is designed to feel psychologically useful while staying legally safer through output discipline.

Rules for user-facing output:

- Describe communication patterns only
- Prefer comparisons over absolute labels
- Do not accuse, diagnose, predict outcomes, or advise major life decisions
- Do not claim or imply truth, deception, fidelity, hidden intent, guilt, innocence, hiring outcome, or rejection outcome
- Do not use implication-heavy verdict phrases such as “this suggests”, “this indicates”, or “this points to” when they lead into identity, motive, or outcome claims
- Keep summaries calm, short, neutral, and structured

Examples of preferred framing:

- “Later replies contain more hedging markers than earlier replies.”
- “Answer specificity softened after the midpoint.”
- “Interview responses showed more hesitation markers in the later segments.”
- “Direct answer style weakens after the midpoint.”

Examples of disallowed framing:

- “They are lying.”
- “This proves cheating.”
- “This suggests hidden intent.”
- “The candidate failed.”
- “Break up with them.”
