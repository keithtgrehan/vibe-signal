# Final Thesis: Minimal Human Product UI

## Chosen UX thesis

Vibe Signal is a calm message-reading aid that helps users stop guessing and look at observable wording before they reply.

## Chosen UI thesis

Use one clean product flow with the demo/result area as the visual center: hero, one synthetic example, evidence-first result, simple analyzer, three-step explanation, and a short trust footer.

## Chosen copy thesis

The copy should feel human and useful: name the uncertainty users feel, offer relief through clearer wording, and avoid fear, shame, urgency, secret-meaning claims, or compliance-heavy language.

## Chosen safety thesis

Push usefulness through observable wording analysis only. Vibe Signal can describe ambiguity, pressure, unclear asks, repair openings, and safer next steps; it cannot claim knowledge of intent, feelings, truthfulness, health labels, attraction, cheating, or outcomes.

## Final page structure

1. Sticky top nav: logo, Demo, How it works, Privacy, and primary Run demo CTA.
2. Hero: headline, subheadline, Run a demo CTA, Analyze text CTA, and one trust line.
3. Demo/result: two columns on desktop and one column on mobile, with one featured synthetic example and the result card as the visual center.
4. Analyze text: textarea, consent checkbox, analyze button, and short helper.
5. How it works: Paste or run a demo, see the evidence, choose a clearer next step.
6. Trust footer: one short boundary sentence and privacy/legal links.

## Final hero copy

Headline: "See what a message is doing - without guessing what someone feels."

Subheadline: "Paste text you’re allowed to use, or run a synthetic demo. Vibe Signal highlights clarity, pressure, unanswered asks, and safer replies."

Trust line: "No mind-reading. No relationship verdicts. Just observable wording."

## Final CTA copy

- Primary: "Run a demo"
- Secondary: "Analyze text"
- Demo card: "Run this demo"
- Analyzer: "Analyze"

## Final result-card structure

1. What stands out
2. Evidence
3. What it could mean
4. Safer reply
5. Limits

Before running: "Run a demo to see what stands out."

Example result:

- What stands out: "The reply answers loosely, but does not confirm Friday."
- Evidence: "maybe later, not sure yet"
- What it could mean: "There may not be a clear decision yet. The safest read is that timing is still open."
- Safer reply: "No worries - can you confirm by Thursday evening so I know whether to keep Friday free?"
- Limits: "This does not tell you what they feel or intend."

## Explicit removals from the current UI

- Goal selector.
- Context selector.
- Analysis style selector.
- First-screen comparison mode.
- Multiple visible demo cards.
- Nested evidence table.
- Repeated trust-chip wall.
- Large can/cannot sections.
- Repeated legalistic safety copy.
- Developer-facing labels like "metadata-only feedback" in primary flow.
